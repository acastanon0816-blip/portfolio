What is Typography?

Typography is the practice of arranging type to make written language readable, clear, and visually attractive. Solid typography makes it easier for users to scan information, understand hierarchy, and ultimately have an enjoyable reading experience.

Key Typography Terms

Typeface
A group or family of fonts with a consistent design (example: Arial).
Font
A particular style of typeface (example: Arial Bold).
Serif
The finishing strokes at the ends of letters.
Sans Serif
Without serif strokes; letters that are often clean and modern.
Kerning
Adjusting the space between specific letter pairs.
Tracking
Adjusting overall letter spacing across a word or paragraph.
Leading
Spacing between lines of text.
Hierarchy
Using size/weight/spacing to highlight or emphasize the text that is most important.
Legibility
The ease with which you can distinguish between individual characters.
Readability
The ease with which you can read and understand longer blocks of text.
